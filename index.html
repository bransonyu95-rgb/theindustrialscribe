Questions you probably have

Honest answers. No sales spin.

I've never used AI before. Will this actually work for me?
Yes — and honestly, this is better for you than for someone who's been dabbling randomly for a year. We start from how manufacturing actually works, not from how AI tools are marketed. You don't need to know what an LLM is. You need to know what your job involves — and you already do. We handle the translation.
Is this confidential? Can I share real work problems here?
This is the question we get most, and it's the right one to ask. The community is closed — no public posts, no LinkedIn sharing, no screenshots circulated outside. You're not required to name your employer, your customers, or your products. Most members share problems at the process level without revealing anything proprietary. B2B manufacturing runs on NDAs and confidentiality. We built The Industrial Scribe around that reality.
I'm a QC Executive / Production Planner / Purchasing Executive — is this relevant for my specific role?
Yes. We have content tracks built around specific manufacturing roles. QC content covers RCA, CAPA, NCR, SOP, audit prep. Production content covers WIP reporting, scheduling tools, capacity summaries. Purchasing content covers supplier comparison, cost tracking, and quotation management. BD content covers costing models, proposal writing, and margin simulation. If your role involves repetitive document work, data compilation, or report writing — there's something here for you.
How is this different from just using ChatGPT on my own?
ChatGPT doesn't know what a PFMEA is. It doesn't understand that your customer requires 8D format. It doesn't know the difference between a rework cost and a scrap cost in your specific costing structure. What you get here is the context layer — how to apply AI tools to manufacturing workflows specifically, with prompts and templates already built for your role. Going it alone with ChatGPT is like having a very smart intern who's never been in a factory. Useful, but limited. We fill that gap.
I'm a full-time executive. How much time does this actually take?
We designed this for people with full-time manufacturing jobs. Realistically: 30–45 minutes a week is enough to make steady progress. The tutorials are structured to get you to a usable result in one sitting. The community is asynchronous — post a question when you have it, check back when you can. If you have 30 minutes on a Sunday evening, that's enough.
